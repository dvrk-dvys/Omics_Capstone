1.1 SONFH Background

Steroid-Induced Osteonecrosis of the Femoral Head (SONFH) is a severe debilitating joint and bone condition that is characterized by reduced blood supply to the hip socket (acetabulum) leading to bone death (osteonecrosis) and even a full joint collapse. The disease is known to be caused by commonly prescribed glucosteroid medication used to treat autoimmune-related and inflammatory diseases such as prednisone for Allergy Induced Asthma and others (citation 1). The condition also can be triggered by reduced blood supply (ischemia), altered lipid metabolism and oxidative stress caused by prolonged alcohol abuse (citation 2). Pharmacological interventions and experimental blood serum transfusion have been promising but without early detection the condition is usually irreversible and requires surgical intervention such as a full joint replacement. Recent research efforts have been able to find promising potential serum biomarkers for early detection but more work is still needed. This paper serves as an exploratory research analysis to showcase robust machine learning and automation techniques for identifying and qualifying biomarkers for SONFH detection.

2.1 Dataset and Preprocessing

As outlined in the Introduction, the GSE123568 dataset was chosen for the sheer amount of probes used and the fact that there were enough samples (30 SONFH and 10 non-SONFH controls), providing both biological relevance and sufficient class representation for supervised machine learning. The GSE123568 was obtained from the NCBI Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO) repository and was generated using microarray technology (Affymetrix Human PrimeView, GPL15207) to detect raw expression data for the genes which those probes targeted. This does not allow for the detection of novel transcripts like RNA-seq would, but given that there is a dearth of public and human patient-level samples data available for this disease, I believe that a microarray panel will suffice for the purposes of this course and this capstone project. The raw files of this study provide an initial matrix of 49,293 probes across 40 samples, after which variance filtering was applied to remove probes with IQR < 0.5 log2 units, resulting in 11,687 retained probes; the data were confirmed as log2-transformed RMA-normalised (range 1.43–14.14) and required no further transformation.

After downloading the input files (GEO series matrix and SOFT files) a series of preprocessing scripts were ran to prepare for the training of the ml models. These scripts reproduce and expand upon the core functionality of the R scripts provided during the course:

Step 1: The Tranpose_Function.R was converted into the parse_series_matrix.py as a stand alone script which parses the GEO series matrix format, extracts sample metadata, then transposes to a Weka acceptable orientation (samples × probes) with class ids appended ("control group" or "SONFH group").

Step 2: The filtering functionality of simple ANN wrapper and filter.R was split into the preprocess.py script which filters out uninformative features but uses different and in some cases less strict filtering to allow for wider and more robust training steps. The new filtering parameters probes with an interquartile range below 0.2 log₂ units across all 40 samples were removed as uninformative. Because GEO series matrix data is already RMA-normalised and log₂-transformed, no additional normalisation was applied. This step reduced the feature space from 49,293 to 45,465 probes, retaining only those with meaningful expression variation.

2.2 Feature Selection Strategies

Writing Guide — Feature Selection (two approaches) — DELETE BEFORE SUBMITTING Describe both approaches and contrast them at the end. The contrast is the key point: hybrid score finds genes that work well collectively; ANN wrapper finds genes that are individually diagnostic. These are different scientific questions, and the combination is more informative than either alone. This contrast sets up your Discussion section on multivariate vs univariate interpretation.

Multivariate (Hybrid Scoring Approach): The ranking and selecting functionality of simple ANN wrapper and filter.R was split into the feature_select.py script which now uses a hybrid ranking score:

Hybrid Score = Z(|FC|) + Z(|t|)

Where FC (fold change) is the difference in mean log₂ expression between SONFH and control groups (FC = μSONFH − μcontrol); t is Welch's t-statistic comparing probe expression between groups; Z(·) denotes z-score normalisation across all probes.

This approach prioritises probes that are both strongly differentially expressed (effect size) and statistically significant across all samples. The top 100 probes were selected for Weka-based classification, while the top 500 probes were retained for the Python-based Omics ML pipeline. Output files were generated in .arff format for direct compatibility with Weka.
